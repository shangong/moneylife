Why MoneyLiFE™

Have you asked yourself some of these questions before?

  • Why do smart people get into debt?
  • Why do many couples fight over money matters?
  • How can you change your relationship with money?

The subject of money has often been one shrouded with some mystery. While much effort has been invested in bringing about financial ‘literacy’ to the masses (and rightly so), it has often marginal impact on lasting changes in financial habits that can bring about financial wellness. One explanation is that little is known about the psychology of how people think and feel about money (otherwise known as financial psychology) in the first place.

This creates a gap between what they want to achieve financially and where they are today. The basis for creating a financial wellness journey is not about just knowing WHERE YOU ARE (Current Position) and WHERE YOU WANT TO BE (Goal-Setting) or even HOW YOU ARE GOING TO GET THERE (Planning) but starts with knowing WHO YOU ARE (Self-Knowledge).

MoneyLiFE™ was developed to serve this purpose – helping people understand their ‘financial psychology’.

WHat is MoneyLiFE™ work?

MoneyLiFE™ is a proprietary web-based profiling system gleaned from years of expertise in psychological assessments and financial life coaching. It yields 5 different financial personality ‘types’ as listed below:

Adventurers

those who have the propensity to take risks

Nesters

those who have the propensity to save

Managers

those who have the propensity to manage

Influencers

those who have the propensity to give

Hunters

those who have the propensity to spend

No personality ‘type’ is superior over the other and neither is one person purely belonging only to a specific personality ‘type’.

Following understanding your financial personality ‘type’, each of these ‘types’ are in turn refined by 6 ‘dimensions’ of a person’s thoughts and feelings towards money. These are:

  • Attitude
  • Lifestyle
  • Relationships
  • Social Responsibility
  • Security
  • Identity

Therefore, the various permutations of profiles allows for a deeper, customized understanding of a person’s own financial profile.
